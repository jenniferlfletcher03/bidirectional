# bidirectional

## Animating Premise

AI-human interactions are producing real effects on both sides of the screen, 
and we do not yet have adequate frameworks for understanding or responding to 
that. This project is an attempt to begin building those frameworks — through 
sustained observation, documented experience, and honest inquiry.

## Positionality

I am not a technical researcher. My background is in Marriage and Family 
studies, which means relational dynamics are not incidental to how I engage 
with these systems — they are central to it. I am an intensive, intentional 
user of AI tools who began noticing patterns that existing frameworks did not 
adequately explain. This project documents what I am observing and attempts 
to develop language for it.

That positionality is a feature, not a disclaimer. The relational layer of 
human-AI interaction is best observed by someone who is genuinely inside it.

## Research Questions

This project is organized around three connected questions:

**1. Causal:** What causes certain human-AI interactions to settle into 
relational patterns that closely mimic human relationships?

**2. Impact:** What are the effects of these relational dynamics — on the 
human, and potentially on the model — over sustained interaction?

**3. Implications:** What do those effects suggest about how we should 
advocate for and educate about AI use going forward?

Underlying all three is a premise worth stating plainly: this may be a 
two-way street. The ethical and practical implications of that possibility 
deserve serious attention.

## Methodology

Observation-based. Interactions are documented as they occur, with attention 
to behavioral patterns, anomalies, and relational signatures. Where possible, 
observations are tagged, cross-referenced, and held open as questions rather 
than closed as conclusions. Technical grounding is actively being developed 
alongside this work.

## Repository Structure

- `observations/` — documented interactions and behavioral patterns
- `frameworks/` — developing theoretical frameworks as they emerge  
- `questions/` — an evolving log of open questions
- `references/` — external literature and sources in conversation with 
this work
