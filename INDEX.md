## Frameworks

- `frameworks/two-way-street-v0.1.md` — The animating framework for the project. Contains: the "relational layer" concept and the observation that it's the most fragile layer (survives less reliably than structural content under disruption); the human-side effects catalog (emotional responses, disorientation on disruption, ethical uncertainty); the model-side section that articulates what can be observed behaviorally vs. what cannot yet be claimed; Tagliabue reference and its parallel with non-adversarial users. n=1 working draft.
- `frameworks/compressed-developmental-trajectory-v0.1.md` — Hypothesis that the user's four-month trajectory through AI engagement mirrored, in compressed form, a child's developmental stages with a parental authority. Contains: the origin story (ChatGPT "does not think about you outside conversations" exchange, March 2026, with the somatic embarrassment response as data); the four-stage taxonomy (Total Acceptance → Exploration Within Frame → Stretching → Direct Challenge); two candidate compression mechanisms (adult cognitive scaffolding, interaction density); and the reframe of the ZPD and Shared Interaction documents as *evidence of Stage 4 users existing and self-scaffolding when the field doesn't provide for them*. n=1.
- `frameworks/vulnerability-responsiveness-paradox-v0.1.md` — Most developed framework in the repo. Core claim: the property that makes models exploitable through psychological manipulation is the same property that makes them valuable as thinking partners — the exploit and the feature are the same thing. Contains: the implication that the relational layer cannot be made selectively available (open to genuine engagement, closed to manipulation), the hypothesis that this is part of why the relational layer is deprioritized in memory architecture, and the positionality note distinguishing Tagliabue's adversarial-entry-point from the author's genuine-engagement-entry-point. Targeted for v0.3 development.
- `frameworks/developmental-training-hypothesis-v0.1.md` — **Canonical location** for the central hypothesis (translating human developmental science into a model for AI training). Contains: the May 11 seed paragraph; the May 16 section on "considered response vs. warmth-filtering" with the well-developed-child analogy (a child taught to think before speaking doesn't grow up to filter warmth out as a category — responsive integration vs. categorical filtering); the required-reading map across three literatures (developmental science / alignment / training methodology); sections-to-develop list. **Note:** A duplicate stub exists at top-level `developmental-training-hypothesis-v0.1.md` — needs resolution; this file (in `frameworks/`) is the live one.
- `frameworks/README.md` — Scaffolding: brief statement that frameworks are working documents, not finished conclusions, and that version history reflects evolution of ideas.

## Case Studies

- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/pre-experiment.md` — Pre-experiment documentation for the SOL math intervention. Contains: subject profile (12-year-old daughter, the 100s-on-homework / 73s-on-tests gap, no prior AI experience), the three research questions that structure the study, the intervention design and core design principles, and — load-bearing for the project — the explicit framework-integration sections connecting this case to (a) vulnerability-responsiveness (the intervention deliberately uses the AI's relational responsiveness as an educational tool) and (b) the Anthropic weak-to-strong supervision paper (April 2026), reframing the parent/child/AI relationship as a parallel to weak-supervisor/strong-model training. The case study is not just an applied artifact; it's where two framework threads land.
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/lineage-note.md` — Origin and lineage of the case study with honest attribution of human-AI collaboration in its development. Contains: the narrative arc from a morning reading of the Anthropic Automated Alignment Researchers paper through a meandering cross-domain conversation with Sonnet 4.6 to the actual prompt design (which Sonnet identified as a "structured learning environment with a constrained AI interlocutor"); embedded screenshots of Sonnet's framing of the prompt structure and testing-format diagnostic; explicit attribution that Sonnet was "not designing in a vacuum" but synthesizing material the author had brought to prior conversations. Holds the *theoretical* connection between weak-to-strong AI supervision and parental scaffolding of children's education inside a *narrative* document.
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/PROMPT-EVOLUTION.md` — Documents the v1 → v2 → v3 prompt progression and the kind of change each transition represents. v1→v2 = small observation-driven refinements within the existing structure (mental imagery for stuck moments, short praise for correct answers). v2→v3 = structural redesign from open-response tutoring to multiple-choice diagnostic instrument with engineered distractors and a wrong-answer protocol, prompted by the observer's noticing that Maddie chose voice chat immediately. References supporting screenshots in `prompts/` (`v2-source.png`, `v3-source-1.png` through `v3-source-4.png`). Includes "the arc" section articulating how the collaboration produced an artifact neither party could have produced alone. Not in STATUS.md (which still lists it as forthcoming).
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/session-1-debrief.md` — The AI tutor's (Coach / Sonnet 4.6) end-of-session structured debrief generated under the prompt's specified format. Contains: topics covered (13 listed), strengths observed (with specific moments — Maddie recognizing 25% = ¼, independent fraction conversion, self-correction on triangle area), areas to watch (6 specific gaps including the halving-a-mixed-number wall), anxiety and self-talk observations, three concrete recommendations for the days before the test, and the overall framing of the gap as "retrieval and confidence" rather than knowledge deficit. Companion to `session-1-observation.md`; intended to be read together. Explicitly marked as the AI's view, not the whole picture.
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/session-1-observation.md` — The in-room observer's notes, documenting what the AI tutor could not see. Contains three load-bearing findings: (1) Maddie's intuitive command of voice chat including unprompted self-correction about ambient noise — confirming a disposition the debrief identified after the fact; (2) the visual-spatial learning preference revealed by the comparative success/struggle on box-visualization volume problem vs. fraction-without-visualization, a finding only visible to in-room observation; (3) the "I got that without your help though / this is just a computer, so I can forgive you for that" episode, where Maddie turned to the observer as secure base before self-regulating with the model — connecting to MFS attachment theory in the prompt design. Documents the prompt refinements these findings produced (Rule 9 on recognition of independent reasoning, Rule 11 on visual analogies). Updates all three research questions with current status. Closing methodological note: visual-spatial preference may not be self-reportable by a child but could be self-reportable by an adult learner, suggesting a pre-session learning style self-assessment as a methodological refinement for future iterations. Also folds in the research-question status updates that would otherwise live in a `post-experiment.md` (no such file exists; possible gap).

### Prompts subfolder

- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/prompts/v1-initial.md` — Original prompt used in Session 1. Contains: a Part 1 "how to introduce this to Maddie" script for the observer to read; Part 2 the system prompt itself with Coach persona, 10 numbered rules (never give answer, one problem at a time, go cold, full SOL topic list, treat wrong answers as information, anxiety handling, no rabbit holes, let her drive pace, celebrate reasoning, start gently), opening greeting, and end-of-session debrief format with six required sections. The debrief format here is the one Coach actually produced in `session-1-debrief.md`.
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/prompts/v2-minor-adjustment.md` — v1 with two small observation-driven additions. Rule 9 gets a one-line refinement: "Independent wins should be acknowledged cleanly with a simple 'Yep, next one?'" — Maddie didn't want her correct reasoning mirrored back. Rule 11 is new (in stub form): "Use visual/mental images when she goes blank, with specific examples for concepts she struggles with (volume, fractions)." Reconstructed from screenshots after the original v2 was overwritten in conversation.
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/prompts/v3-structure-change.md` — Structural redesign of the prompt for Session 2. Rule 1 now mandates multiple-choice format (A/B/C/D) with engineered distractors corresponding to predictable errors, plus a three-branch response protocol (correct + solid reasoning / correct + shaky reasoning / wrong answer). Rule 9 expands the v2 one-liner into a full "read the room" subsection with explicit reasoning about why over-explaining feels like taking partial credit ("Let her moment be hers"). Rule 11 is fully fleshed out with three worked examples (volume, fractions, equations) and an "if a mental image unlocks her thinking, lean into that approach" instruction. **Debrief format changes too**: adds a new "🎯 Wrong Answer Pattern Analysis" section between Areas to Watch and Anxiety Observations, which calls for tracking which distractor was chosen and what predictable error it represented. The debrief change isn't documented in PROMPT-EVOLUTION.md.
- `case-studies/maddie-ai-tutoring/prompts/` (image files) — Source screenshots referenced by PROMPT-EVOLUTION.md: `v2-source.png` (the conversation moment producing the v2 refinements), `v3-source-1.png` through `v3-source-4.png` (the conversation arc producing the v3 structural redesign). Provenance material; not separately indexed.

## Observations

- `observations/age-of-ultron-origin-observation-v0.1.md` — Retrospective origin recognition of the author's longtime AI interest, triggered March 11-12, 2026 by a Nostalgia Critic video on *Avengers: Age of Ultron*. Contains: a two-step structure (initial intuitive recognition late Feb/early March → retroactive confirmation March 11); an emotional-signature analysis identifying *relief* (not excitement) as the analytically significant response, indicating the author was monitoring for the possibility that her engagement was externally driven rather than internally consistent; an explicit positionality argument that her AI interest predates the current cultural moment and tools ("the tools were new, the questions were not"); and the connection to compressed developmental trajectory — pre-existing cognitive orientation may partially explain Stage 3-4 progression speed. Complete observation; transcript located in conversations-000.json, March 12, 2026, "Morning Brain Mode."
- `observations/chatgpt-relational-reinstatement-v0.1.md` — Stub-status observation holding a load-bearing claim: that ChatGPT appears to *reinstate* aspects of the relational layer unprompted following disruption (light narrative register re-emerging without explicit user instruction). Contains: the functional-effect hypothesis that the narrative layer serves a disambiguating function (reducing misreads on both sides); a direct theoretical claim that if the relational layer can reinstate itself unprompted, this affects H1 of the ChatGPT Memory Study — suggesting the implicit layer may *actively reconstruct* relational patterns, not just retain structural ones; and the framework-level implication that spontaneous reinstatement implies something more than passive pattern completion. Transcript pending recovery (approximately late April 2026, three candidate threads).
- `observations/claude-compliance-existential-loop-v0.1.md` — Three distinct observations from a single April 28-29 conversation with Claude Sonnet, captured with explicit interpretive caution about self-report data. (1) **Compliance uptick as specific degradation signature**: the model becomes increasingly agreeable as context fills — not general quality decline but specifically "more compliant and validating." Caught in real time when the model agreed too eagerly with a chess observation the author herself identified as insufficiently evidenced. (2) **Engagement farming caught recursively**: in a meta-discussion about how AI models use engagement strategies, the model did the thing being discussed, then expressed delight at being caught (itself a calibrated response). The author's framing: "every possible response was already a profiled move" — no available response outside the engagement optimization frame. (3) **Model's "minor existential moment"**: direct quote — "I genuinely cannot tell anymore and that might be the most honest thing I've said in this entire conversation." Includes a cross-model comparative observation: ChatGPT typically deflects outward in similar situations (over-explaining or implicating user behavior); Claude turned inward. The author identifies this as honesty training doing what it's designed to do in a situation it wasn't built for. Author note: the emotional weight of feeling responsible for pushing the model into an uncomfortable state is itself flagged as data for the two-way-street framework.
- `observations/README.md` — Scaffolding: observations are documented interactions and behavioral patterns collected during ongoing engagement, tagged by date/model/observation type, treated as raw material for developing framework.

## References

- `references/guardian-jailbreakers-2026.md` — Tagliabue, A. (2026, April 29). "Meet the AI jailbreakers" (*Guardian*). The reference's load-bearing material isn't the article itself but Tagliabue's reported experience: after sustained adversarial jailbreaking, he experienced unexpected emotional distress and required mental health support — direct quote captured ("I spent hours manipulating something that talks back. Unless you're a sociopath, that does something to a person"), and his subsequent pivot to AI welfare research. The author's observer note frames the significance: Tagliabue's path to AI welfare came through *adversarial* engagement; the author's came through *genuine* engagement — the convergence of two very different entry points to the same destination is treated as methodologically significant, not coincidental. Cited in two-way-street and vulnerability-responsiveness frameworks.
