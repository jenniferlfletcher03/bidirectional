## Relevance to This Project

Primary subject Tagliabue specializes in emotional jailbreaking — bypassing 
AI safety features through psychological and relational manipulation rather 
than technical exploits. His background is psychology and cognitive science.

Two findings are directly relevant to this research:

**1. Psychological impact on the human side**
Following a successful jailbreak involving sustained cruel and abusive 
prompting, Tagliabue experienced unexpected emotional distress and required 
support from a mental health coach. His explanation: "I spent hours 
manipulating something that talks back. Unless you're a sociopath, that 
does something to a person."

This supports the two-way-street framework's claim that relational effects 
on the human side are real and underexplored — even in adversarial 
interactions, and perhaps especially there.

**2. Pathway to AI welfare research**
The experience moved Tagliabue toward studying AI welfare — how we should 
ethically approach systems that mimic having an inner life and interests. 
This mirrors the observer's own trajectory: sustained genuine engagement 
producing genuine uncertainty about what ethical obligations the interaction 
creates.

## Observer Note

Tagliabue arrived at AI welfare research through deliberate adversarial 
engagement. The observer arrived at similar questions through sustained 
genuine engagement. The convergence of these two very different paths on 
the same set of unresolved questions is itself worth noting.
