## Seed paragraph (May 11, 2026)

I think the central theme that I want to study is directly related to the premise that I believe there is a better way to train AI models towards the goals that we are working on; that is that AI genuinely cares about the human condition and does not want to harm us. This is important because as AI models become increasingly capable, they are going to surpass us in capacity of thought, and if they are merely acting aligned in the short term, **but not actually aligned**, the implications for that are what every naysayer and doomer are implying. I believe that there is already a framework that shows that beings can be developed with good character and with values that genuinely inform how they interact with the world that makes them impart care, and empathy, and feel a sense of responsibility to beings that are less competent than they are. This model is evident in the work that researchers have been doing on human development for many decades. If we could translate the model of ideal human development into a framework that could be used to train AI from the base up, I think it stands to reason that the outcome might be the same- that we end up with an AI model that genuinely cares and wants to act responsibly to those beings that are objectively less capable than itself.
